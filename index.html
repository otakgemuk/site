The Business Hierarchy

The Trader's Core Pillars

Defining the distinction between your business rules, your statistical edge, and your historical proof.

Foundation

Trading Plan

The logistics and rules governing your entire business. Covers risk management (daily loss limits), routines, platform settings, and psychological guardrails.

Logic

Strategies

Your specific "if-then" engines for identifying a statistical edge. Defined entry triggers, stop-loss logic, and target placement for specific setups like M2B or H2.

Execution

Playbook

A curated manual of your best-performing setups. While a strategy is a set of rules, a playbook is a visual gallery of those rules working perfectly in real market conditions.

Forensics

Case Studies

Deep-dive reviews of past sessions or specific trades. Historical proof that extracts lessons from mistakes and reinforces confidence in your playbook logic.

About

Sire Mammat

I trade futures — primarily ES, NQ, MES, and MNQ — using a price action approach built on years of study, screen time, and the kind of losses that teach more than wins ever could.

My approach is rooted in Al Brooks' price action methodology combined with volume profile analysis and disciplined session-based execution. I document everything — not to look good, but because the discipline of logging forces honesty.

This site is my public trading journal, education hub, and resource library. If you're serious about learning to trade futures properly, everything here is built to help you cut years off the learning curve — and it's free, always.
