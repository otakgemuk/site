Overview

What Is Sire-GreenLight Core?

Sire-GreenLight Core is a trend-continuation momentum indicator built for index futures traders. It identifies high-probability entries by tracking consecutive directional bars against a 20 EMA bias and filtering through four independent confirmation layers.

The indicator produces three signal types — L8/B8 PRO (highest conviction), L4/B4 (early warning), and Gap 20 EMA (continuation alert) — each representing a different level of momentum confirmation.

Signals

The Three Signal Types

Each signal type fires under different momentum conditions. PRO signals are the highest conviction. Early signals give you a head start. Gap alerts catch momentum breakouts.

🟢
L8 / B8 PRO
High Conviction Entry

Fires when 8 consecutive directional bars align with the 20 EMA bias and pass all four filters.

L8 = 8 bullish bars above 20 EMA (buy)
B8 = 8 bearish bars below 20 EMA (sell)

🔵
L4 / B4 Early
Early Warning

Fires when 4 consecutive directional bars appear with EMA alignment. A precursor to a potential L8/B8.

L4 = 4 bullish bars (watch for L8)
B4 = 4 bearish bars (watch for B8)

⚠
Gap 20 EMA
Continuation Alert

Fires when price gaps significantly away from the 20 EMA after a trend has established. Indicates strong momentum continuation.

Logic

The 4-Filter System

Every signal must pass through four independent filters before it fires. This layered confirmation system eliminates low-quality signals.

● Filter 1 — Consecutive Bar Count
The indicator counts consecutive directional bars. PRO signals require 8 consecutive bars. Early signals require 4. This ensures the momentum is sustained, not a single-bar spike.
● Filter 2 — 20 EMA Bias Alignment
All signals must align with the 20 EMA directional bias. Bullish signals only fire when price is above the 20 EMA. Bearish signals only fire when price is below.
● Filter 3 — Volume Confirmation
Signals are filtered against relative volume. Higher volume on the consecutive bars adds conviction. Low-volume signals are suppressed or downgraded.
● Filter 4 — Price Action Structure
The indicator checks that each consecutive bar is making higher highs / higher lows (bullish) or lower highs / lower lows (bearish). This confirms structured momentum, not choppy consolidation.
Reading Guide

How to Use Sire-GreenLight Core

Step 1 — Check the 20 EMA bias. Is price above or below? This determines which direction you should be looking for signals.

Step 2 — Watch for L4/B4 early warnings. When 4 consecutive bars align with the EMA, start preparing. The trend is building.

Step 3 — Wait for L8/B8 PRO confirmation. When 8 consecutive bars pass all four filters, this is your entry signal.

Step 4 — Monitor Gap 20 EMA alerts. If price gaps away from the EMA after a PRO signal, the trend is accelerating.

Step 5 — Exit when the consecutive bar count resets or when price crosses back through the 20 EMA.

Conditions

When It Works Best

● Ideal Conditions
Trending markets — the indicator is built for momentum continuation
5-minute chart — primary execution timeframe
Index futures — MNQ, MES, ES, NQ
Active sessions — NY and London overlap for best volume
● Avoid
Range-bound markets — consecutive bar counts reset frequently in chop
Low volume periods — lunch hour, pre-market, holidays
1-minute chart — too noisy
FOMC / news events — volatility spikes break the filter logic
